Construction of the Comensals zine & MDEFest active exhibition

A reflection on what the making taught me, and where it wants to go next.

Going into this, the parts I was most unsure about were the handmade ones. The paper especially. I kept thinking it wasn’t finished enough, that the sheets were too uneven, the colours didn’t match, the edges were rough, and that people would see it as something half-done rather than something made on purpose. There’s a kind of perfectionism that tells you the work isn’t ready to be shown.. and with the paper, and honestly with the drawings too, I spent a lot of time circling that feeling instead of just letting the thing be what it was.

What I didn’t expect was that the paper would be the part people responded to most. The rough, uneven, orange-peel sheets were the thing people wanted to pick up and hold, the thing they were drawn to.. the exact thing I’d been quietly apologising for in my head. It caught me off guard.

That shifted something for me. The unfinished quality wasn’t a compromise I was making because I couldn’t do better.. it was the argument the whole project is making. Comensals is about composting, about things being valuable precisely because they’re breaking down and imperfect and made by hand, and somewhere along the way I’d been holding the work to a standard that contradicts its own point. The rough sheets are the thesis, not a failure to reach it.

on its own clock

Letting the work be made the way it talks about

The other thing I learned is about how I work, and how the work wanted to be made. The zine talks about phytocrip.. the tenderness toward those of us who can’t be rushed on an anthropocentric, productive timeline, the plant that blooms when it’s ready, the nervous system that’s “too sensitive”. And at some point it stopped making sense to make a project about that on a workflow that didn’t honour it. The slowness wasn’t a problem to manage, it was the thing the zine was actually about. So I tried to let the making run on its own clock.. the paper that can’t dry faster than it dries, the drawings that came when they came, my own rhythm included.

That carried into the activation too. Before people did the lino cutting, they sat with the question “what would you like to decompose?”, and it was nice to watch them slow down.. a lot of them needed a real moment before they could answer, which is the point, the pause is part of the composting. If someone got stuck or didn’t quite get it, I’d tell them to look through the zine first to get the vibe, and that was usually enough.. the zine did the explaining so I didn’t have to. People arrived at it in their own time, which felt like the whole thing working.

I don’t think I’d make it cleaner if I did it again. The mess was the part that taught me the most, and it’s the part that ended up mattering to other people too.

next phase

Past the zine, toward something collective

What I want next is for this to keep going past the zine. The whole project has been pointing toward something collective.. the compost communities, the conversations with Vinik, the idea that this isn’t really a solo object but a proposition for people to gather around. I’d love to work with BIOMA, or to start a collective of my own, and grow Comensals into the kind of recurring gathering it’s been describing all along.. people meeting to bring their scraps and their grief and compost something together, the pile as the excuse and the symbol.

Some of that is about me catching up to the work. I get anxious presenting, and that’s something I want to get better at rather than avoid, because a project that’s about gathering people sort of requires being able to stand in front of them. And I want to keep building my Spanish, since the future I’m imagining is here, in Barcelona, in this territory and these communities specifically. So the next phase is partly the project growing, and partly me growing into the parts of it I’m not fluent in yet.. literally and otherwise.

Come to the table. Bring your scraps and your grief. Let us rot together.
